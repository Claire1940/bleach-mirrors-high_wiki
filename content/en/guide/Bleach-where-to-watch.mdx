<Section icon={Map} title="Bleach Chronological & Release Watch Order">

To fully appreciate Ichigo Kurosaki's journey from a high school student to a powerful Soul Reaper, watching the series in the correct narrative sequence is essential. The story transitions from localized supernatural mysteries in Karakura Town to massive spiritual wars across the Soul Society, Hueco Mundo, and the Quincy empire. 

For viewers who want to experience the narrative exactly as author Tite Kubo intended, following the chronological progression of the canonical arcs is highly recommended. 

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The Substitute Soul Reaper Arc">
    Episodes 1 to 20. This introductory arc establishes the core rules of the spiritual world. Ichigo Kurosaki accidentally absorbs Rukia Kuchiki's powers, learns to wield a Zanpakuto, battles local Hollows, and meets key allies like Uryu Ishida and Orihime Inoue.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Soul Society Arc">
    Episodes 21 to 63. Widely considered one of the greatest shonen arcs of all time. Ichigo and his friends invade the Soul Society to rescue Rukia from execution, forcing them to fight the elite Captains of the Gotei 13 and uncover a massive conspiracy led by Sosuke Aizen.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="The Arrancar & Hueco Mundo Arcs">
    Episodes 110 to 316 (excluding anime-original fillers). This massive saga details Aizen's creation of the Arrancar army and the Espada. The conflict escalates into a full-scale rescue mission in the desert world of Las Noches and concludes with the decisive battle in a replicated Karakura Town.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Lost Agent Arc">
    Episodes 343 to 366. Following the loss of his Soul Reaper abilities, a vulnerable Ichigo encounters the Fullbringer organization known as Xcution. This arc explores the psychological toll of Ichigo's sacrifices and restores his powers just in time for the final war.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Thousand-Year Blood War Arc">
    The modern sequel series. It adapts the final manga volumes (55-74), detailing the brutal war between the Gotei 13 and Yhwach's hidden Quincy empire, the Wandenreich.
  </Step>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Thousand-Year Blood War (TYBW) Streaming Details">

The return of Bleach in the form of the Thousand-Year Blood War (TYBW) represents a massive leap forward in production value. Produced by Studio Pierrot, this sequel replaces the long-running weekly format with high-budget, seasonal "cours" (parts). It features modern cinematic lighting, fluid choreography, and significantly less censorship than the original series, allowing the brutal nature of the Quincy conflict to be fully realized.

Because of this premium production style, the streaming rights are strictly controlled. You will not find TYBW on standard anime platforms like Crunchyroll or retro-focused streaming sites.
